About Me

I am originally from the Philippines. My maternal grandmother had me petitioned to move and live here in America, for better opportunities and to broaden my horizons.I moved when I was 20 years old and the only English I knew was from books. I wasn't aware that speaking it would be so different from learning it.

To me, learning a new language is fascinating. And that is one of the many reasons why I have chosen to take this course.

I have been in different jobs and in different places. I have listed my professional experience in order, starting from my very first job at 17 years old up to now.

  1. Telemarketer at one of the local branches of a National FastFood Restaurant Chain
  2. Janitor at Taco Bell in Fresno, California when I first moved here in the U.S., and couldn't speak or understand conversational English.
  3. In Home Nanny (this is the time that I started really teaching myself how to speak without or at least, with a lot less Filipino accent) in San Bernardino, California.
  4. Joined the U.S. Military for a short time, Army branch. I worked as an Administrative Assistant, in several different posts.
  5. Certified Nurse Assistant and Home Health Aide.
  6. Restorative Nurse Assistant where I worked closely with Physical and Occupational Therapists
  7. Surgical Nurse Assistant at Fresno Heart Hospital.
  8. Hospice Nurse Assistant in Fresno, California.

After a major car accident: I moved here in Portland Oregon to start anew, and found a job as a Barista. I also found employment in the retail industry, in different positions. Currently, I am not working and just focusing in school.

In my free time, I like to draw portraits, play and watch other people play video games, and walking around and shopping downtown with my best friend, Fredrick.
